How the SPARK Intake Process Works

Stage 1: Idea Capture

Ideas can come from anyone — parents, teachers, community members, students, SPARK team members, or through SUSD's periodic landscape scan of educational trends. All ideas enter through the same Intake Form for consistent evaluation.

Stage 2: Intake Form

A structured form (15-20 minutes) captures the essential concept, strategic alignment, demand signals, instructional model, feasibility, and cost considerations. Each section maps to SUSD's Quality Indicators (QI), ensuring every idea is evaluated on the same criteria used for existing programs.

Stage 3: Preliminary Screen

SPARK leadership performs a quick review: Is this a duplicate? Is there an obvious blocker? Does it have minimum viability? Ideas either PASS to triage, are PARKED in the registry for future consideration, or are REDIRECTED to an existing program.

Stage 4: SPARK Triage

The SPARK core team evaluates screened concepts using the Preliminary Alignment Scorecard — a lightweight rubric with 6 categories (18 criteria) mapped to Quality Indicators. Concepts scoring well advance to Deep Review; promising but incomplete ideas return for more development.

Stage 5: Deep Review

Advanced concepts are assigned to a SPARK Design Team (for new concepts) or Review Team (for existing program adjustments) for full evaluation using the complete QI rubric and evidence framework.

Stage 6: Decision & Recommendation

The SPARK team makes a formal recommendation: Approve for pilot, Approve for design phase, Defer (with auto re-surface), or Decline (with documented rationale). All decisions are transparent and tracked.

The Idea Registry

Every idea — regardless of stage — lives in a central registry. Nothing falls through the cracks. Deferred ideas are automatically re-surfaced. Patterns emerge over time (e.g., if 10 families ask about environmental science, that's a signal). The registry is reviewed at every quarterly SPARK meeting.

Periodic Landscape Scan

Quarterly, SPARK reviews open enrollment trends, nearby district/charter/private program launches, national education trends, and workforce signals. This scan feeds directly into the Idea Registry as "landscape-sourced" entries — ensuring SUSD stays ahead of the market.
