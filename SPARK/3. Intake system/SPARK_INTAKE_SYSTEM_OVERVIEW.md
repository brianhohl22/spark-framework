# SPARK Program Intake System — Overview

## Purpose
The SPARK Intake System provides a structured, transparent, and repeatable process for capturing new program ideas from any source and routing them through consistent screening, evaluation, and decision-making. It ensures that no good idea gets lost between quarterly meetings and that every concept is evaluated against the same Quality Indicators (QI) that govern existing programs.

## Design Principles
1. **Lightweight entry, rigorous evaluation** — submitting an idea should be easy; the system adds rigor progressively
2. **Aligned to Quality Indicators** — every stage maps back to SUSD's QI framework
3. **Transparent and communicable** — any stakeholder can see where an idea stands and why
4. **Feeds existing SPARK workstreams** — not a parallel process; it plugs into Program Review and Design teams
5. **Supports the periodic landscape scan** — intake data becomes intelligence for SPARK's strategic view

## Process Flow (6 Stages)

```
[1. IDEA CAPTURE] → [2. INTAKE FORM] → [3. PRELIMINARY SCREEN] → [4. SPARK TRIAGE] → [5. DEEP REVIEW] → [6. DECISION]
     ↑                                        ↓ (park/redirect)          ↓ (assign team)
  Multiple                               [IDEA REGISTRY]            [Design Team or
  Entry Points                           (tracked, visible)          Review Team]
```

### Stage 1: Idea Capture (Entry Points)
Ideas can arrive from multiple sources — the system captures them all consistently:

| Entry Point | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Community / Family** | Parents, community members, local organizations | "We need a Mandarin immersion option" |
| **Educators / Staff** | Teachers, coordinators, principals with classroom insight | "Our STEM elective could become a full pathway" |
| **Innovation Grants** | Concepts emerging from SUSD Innovation Startup Grants | Grant-funded pilot showing strong results |
| **CTE Pathways** | Ideas surfacing through Career & Technical Education | BOP entrepreneurship → student innovation lab |
| **SPARK Team** | Ideas generated in quarterly meetings or subgroups | Sticky-note ideas from the Jan 2026 launch |
| **Landscape Scan** | Trends spotted in competitor/charter/private school offerings | Nearby district launching AI & Robotics academy |
| **Student Voice** | Student councils, surveys, or direct feedback | "We want a film & media production program" |

### Stage 2: Intake Form
A structured form captures the essential information about a new program idea. Fields are designed to be completable by anyone (parent, teacher, admin) in 15-20 minutes.

**See: `SPARK_New_Program_Intake_Form.md`**

### Stage 3: Preliminary Screen (Administrative)
SPARK leadership (Ms. Mitchell or designee) performs a quick screen:
- Is this a **duplicate** of an existing program or idea already in the registry?
- Is there an **obvious blocker** (e.g., legal, facility, or policy constraint)?
- Does it have **minimum viability** (at least a plausible student demand and delivery model)?

**Outcome**: PASS (→ Triage) | PARK (→ Registry with reason) | REDIRECT (→ existing program/channel)

### Stage 4: SPARK Triage (Quarterly or As-Needed)
The SPARK core team reviews screened concepts using the **Preliminary Alignment Scorecard** — a lightweight rubric mapping to QI categories:

| QI Category | Triage Question | Quick Score |
|---|---|---|
| Mission & Strategic Alignment | Does this advance SUSD's strategic plan? | High / Medium / Low |
| Student Demand & Access | Is there evidence of demand? Who is served? | High / Medium / Low |
| Instructional Quality | Is the pedagogical approach sound? | High / Medium / Low |
| Operational Feasibility | Can we realistically deliver this? | High / Medium / Low |
| Financial Sustainability | Is the cost model viable? | High / Medium / Low |
| Community & Stakeholder Alignment | Does this reflect community needs? | High / Medium / Low |

**Outcome**: ADVANCE to Deep Review | DEVELOP FURTHER (return with more info) | ARCHIVE (with rationale)

### Stage 5: Deep Review
Concepts that advance are assigned to a SPARK Design Team or Review Team for full evaluation, using the complete QI rubric and evidence framework.

### Stage 6: Decision & Recommendation
SPARK team makes a formal recommendation to district leadership:
- **Approve for pilot** (with implementation plan)
- **Approve for design phase** (needs more development)
- **Defer** (good concept, wrong timing)
- **Decline** (with documented rationale)

## Idea Registry
All ideas — regardless of stage — are tracked in a central registry so:
- Nothing falls through the cracks between quarterly meetings
- SPARK can see the full pipeline at any time
- Patterns emerge (e.g., 5 families asked about environmental science → signal)
- Deferred ideas are automatically re-surfaced on a cadence

## Periodic Landscape Scan
Quarterly, SPARK reviews:
- Open enrollment trends (where are families going?)
- Nearby district/charter/private program launches
- National/state educational program trends
- Workforce and industry signals (via CTE and community partnerships)

This scan feeds directly into the Idea Registry as "landscape-sourced" entries.
