Structured Intake

All ideas enter through a consistent Intake Form — a 15-minute structured questionnaire aligned to SUSD's Quality Indicators. This ensures every concept is captured with enough detail for fair screening.

Preliminary Screen

SPARK leadership performs a quick check: Is this a duplicate? Is there an obvious blocker? Does it meet a minimum viability threshold? Ideas that pass move to Evaluate. Others are parked in the registry (tracked, not lost) or redirected to an existing program.

Periodic Landscape Scan

Quarterly, SPARK reviews: open enrollment trends, nearby district/charter/private program launches, national education trends, and workforce signals. Findings feed directly into the Idea Registry.

Framework › Evaluate

Evaluate — How SPARK Assesses

SPARK uses consistent, QI-aligned evaluation for both new concepts entering the pipeline and existing programs under periodic review. The same quality standards apply across the board.

New Concept Triage

The Preliminary Alignment Scorecard evaluates new ideas across 6 QI categories (18 criteria, 54 points). Concepts scoring well advance to deep review.

Mission & Strategic Alignment/9
Student Demand & Access/9
Instructional Quality/9
Operational Feasibility/9
Financial Sustainability/9
Community & Stakeholder/9

Existing Program Review

Annual health check using the full QI rubric + evidence framework. The Program Review sub-team conducts deep evaluations and produces both public-facing summaries and detailed internal annexes.

Current reviews: MSA (Math/Science Academy) and STEM programs under active evaluation by the Program Review sub-team.

Deep Review

Concepts that advance from triage are assigned to a SPARK Design Team (new concepts) or Review Team (existing programs). These sub-teams conduct comprehensive evaluation using the full Quality Indicator rubric, evidence packs, and stakeholder input.

Act — How SPARK Decides & Delivers

Implementation & Monitoring

Approved programs receive tiered implementation plans with measurable goals, designated program leads, and capacity-building support (staffing, funding, professional learning). SPARK monitors progress and reports annually to district leadership.

Communication & Stakeholder Engagement

Every decision comes with a clear "why." SPARK supports transparent communication to families and community — sharing value, purpose, and progress of specialty programs across the district.
